  1. Engine version matters

  • Godot 4: PathFollow2D no longer uses offset. Use progress_ratio (0..1) or progress (pixels).

$MobPath/MobSpawnLocation.offset = randf() * $MobPath.curve.get_baked_length()

  1. randi() is the issue

  • bound it to the curve length: progress = randf() * $MobPath.curve.get_baked_length().

    mob_spawn_location must be the PathFollow2D node, not the Path2D:

@onready var mob_spawn_location: PathFollow2D = $MobPath/MobSpawnLocation

Do that and your spawns will distribute uniformly along the path without weird jumps.
